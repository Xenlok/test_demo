    I am Director of the "Laboratorio de Neurociencia", Assistant Professor at the Business School at Universidad Torcuato Di Tella, and a researcher at the National Scientific and Technical Research Council (CONICET) of Argentina. With a background in Physics (University of Buenos Aires) and a PhD in Neuroscience (University of Leicester), I have worked in several lines of research related to cognitive sciences, experimental psychology, and human behavioral sciences. Lately, I have been focused on understanding how people make judgements and decisions in social settings, how they change their minds following social influence, and how this processes relate to political polarization. To study these topics, I perform and analyze experiments conducted in large-scale public engagement events, online platforms, and in the lab.
